How a Toronto Restaurant Filled 40% More Tables with One Postcard

GM

Gabriel Macovei

Founder, Spotix

Weeknight tables sit empty. That's the quiet crisis most independent restaurants face between Monday and Thursday. For Maria's Trattoria, a 48-seat Italian spot near Davisville, the problem was getting worse every month.

The Challenge

Maria's had built a loyal weekend crowd through word-of-mouth, but weeknights were unpredictable. They tried Instagram ads — $600 spent, 12 clicks, zero reservations tracked. The feed was crowded, the algorithm unpredictable, and the audience wasn't truly local.

"We were throwing money into a black box," said Marco, the owner. "I couldn't tell if a single person who saw our ad ever walked through the door."

The Spotix Approach

Marco booked a Duo Spot ($499) targeting two postal codes within a 10-minute walk of the restaurant. His offer was simple: "Free appetizer with any entree — Monday through Thursday."

We designed the layout using his existing logo and a food photo he shot on his phone. The postcard included a QR code linking to a reservation page and a unique phone number for call-in bookings. Proof approved in 24 hours. Printed and mailed by the 1st of the month.

The Results

Within three weeks of the 10,000-home mailing, Marco had concrete numbers:

  • 87 QR scans from the postcard
  • 34 call-in reservations via the unique tracking number
  • 40% increase in weeknight table occupancy vs. previous month
  • $8.40 cost per reservation — compared to $50+ for previous digital attempts

Why It Worked

The postcard didn't compete with a hundred other posts in a feed. It sat on a kitchen counter while someone made dinner plans. The offer was hyper-local — these were neighbors who could realistically walk to the restaurant. And the tracking proved what worked, so Marco knew to renew for month two.

The Follow-Up

Marco renewed for month two with a slightly different offer — a prix fixe menu for weeknights. He's now on a 3-month subscription with the 15% discount. "This is the first marketing channel where I can see exactly what I spent and exactly what I got back," he told us.
